The idea for this site started back in 2020. I found that I enjoyed taking difficult to understand topics and write user documentation about how to use it. I’m not exactly the fastest person in the room when it comes to learning new topics, but I found I’ve been able to leverage this ability on the positive side to document and explain the topic to the average user and various stakeholders. From feedback received internally from my times at various offices, user reviews have been positive. So, when the opportunity presented itself, I would create user manuals and other documentation related to my department and projects to better explain how to perform certain tasks.

The images and graphics used in these documents were created by using Inkscape. I like to think that besides having a typical user manual with lines to text to explain how to perform a task, I find the use of images has helped tremendously to ‘get the point across’ to the audience. Maybe like an Ikea instruction manual but with more text. For most manuals and documents I have created at work, Microsoft PowerPoint is used. Yes,that is correct. A little unorthodox, but I found the flexibility of creating page layouts, custom formats and other tools to my liking. Plus, I didn’t have to create a purchase request for a software license, go through layers bureaucracy, and security approvals to try and get an editing program.

Important: The documents presented here are of my own knowledge and work. Though not directly involved with some of the Data Center activities written in this repository, their subject matter is from self-education, watching on-site operations performing the tasks, and training over the years.


Technical writing is an undervalued yet most valuable skill that all people in all industries can have. If you want to become known for your work, you must become good at technical writing. A really good product is no good if you can’t explain why is it good.
